07 — Three ways to structure it

Pick the split that fits.

Same division in all three — the only difference is how we split what it earns. From a pure contractor split where I take zero salary, to salary-first where Vedda keeps nearly every dollar. These are example structures — we settle the exact numbers together.

PLAN 1

The Contractor

$0
base salary
60 / 40
split of net profit — me / Vedda
You get
A whole division with zero payroll. You invest the resources — tools, software, workspace, marketing runway — and take 40% of the division's net profit after business expenses, plus the internal automation and the print work AI clients bring.
I do
Everything, as an independent contractor. Build every system, run the division day to day, sell it under the Vedda name, and deliver every client — on my own risk.
Why it costs this
I take no salary at all — my entire income rides on the division working. The 60/40 reflects who's carrying that risk. You're investing resources, not wages: if it earns nothing, you've paid no one.
Napkin math, yr 1
~$290k of AI revenue, ~$250k after expenses: Vedda takes ~$100k with zero payroll spent · I take ~$150k and cover myself.
★ Recommended — the balanced middle
